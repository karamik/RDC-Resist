# Contributing to RDC-Resist

**Thank you for your interest.** However, this repository is **not an open-source project** and does not accept unsolicited contributions.

RDC-Resist is a proprietary pre‑commercial technology protected by provisional patents and trade secrets. All content is provided for evaluation purposes only under the terms of the [LICENSE](./LICENSE).

---

## For External Researchers, Engineers, or Companies

If you represent a research institution (e.g., IMEC, ARCNL, CEA‑Leti), a semiconductor manufacturer (e.g., ASML, Intel, TSMC, Samsung), or a chemical supplier (e.g., JSR, TOK, DuPont, Inpria) and wish to propose a collaboration, joint development, or licensing agreement, please follow this process:

### 1. Initial Contact
Reach out via the channels listed in the [README](./README.md) or below. No technical details are required in the first message — only a brief statement of intent and your institutional affiliation.

- **Email:** `totalprotocol@proton.me`
- **Telegram:** `@tec_support_bot`

### 2. Sign a Mutual NDA
After initial discussion, we will provide a standard **Mutual Non‑Disclosure Agreement** (simple, two‑page). Signing the NDA is mandatory before any technical exchange.

### 3. Receive Technical Abstract & Data Package
Once the NDA is in place, you will receive:
- Full simulation results (Monte Carlo electron trajectories)
- Synthetic protocols for the RDEA‑active clusters (without critical parameters)
- Pilot wafer test reports (LWR, defect density, dose matrices)

### 4. Joint R&D or License Negotiation
Based on the data, we will discuss:
- Joint development at IMEC/ARCNL (TRL 5 → 6)
- Exclusive or non‑exclusive licensing terms
- Acquisition or joint venture options

## What We Do NOT Accept

- ❌ **Pull requests** — directly editing files in this repository is forbidden.
- ❌ **Issues** with technical commentary or reverse‑engineering attempts.
- ❌ **Forks** that modify or redistribute the technology.
- ❌ **Unsolicited proposals** from individuals without a verifiable semiconductor industry affiliation.

All such actions will be considered a violation of the license and may result in legal notice.

---

## Code of Conduct (for Authorised Collaborators Only)

If you become a signed partner, a separate **Joint Development Agreement** will define conduct, IP ownership, and publication rules. Until then, no community standards apply beyond the strict prohibition of unauthorised use.

---

*This repository is a controlled document. The only accepted contribution is a signed legal agreement followed by direct collaboration — not GitHub commits.*
