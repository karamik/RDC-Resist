# Cost‑Benefit Analysis: RDC-Resist vs. Conventional MOR
## For a 40k wafers/month Giga‑Fab (High‑NA EUV line)

*All figures in USD, based on public equipment costs and typical fab operating models.*

---

### Assumptions

| Parameter | Conventional MOR | RDC‑Resist |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Dose‑to‑clear (E₀) | 70 mJ/cm² | 40 mJ/cm² |
| LWR (3σ) | 1.4 nm | 0.95 nm |
| Stochastic defect density D₀ | 0.5 cm⁻² | <0.05 cm⁻² |
| Yield on 1.4 nm node | 45% | 85% |
| Resist cost per liter | $2,000 | $4,000 (initial) → $2,500 (ramp) |
| Wafers processed per hour per scanner | 95 | 220 |
| Number of scanners required for 40k wafers/month | 8.8 ≈ 9 | 3.8 ≈ 4 |

---

### 1. Lithography Equipment Cost Savings

- **Cost per High‑NA scanner (EXE:5200B):** $400M  
- **Baseline fleet:** 9 scanners → total CAPEX $3.6B  
- **RDC fleet:** 4 scanners → total CAPEX $1.6B  

**Savings:** **$2.0B** in scanner CAPEX alone (or opportunity to increase output with same fleet).

---

### 2. Yield‑Related Wafer Scrap Reduction

Monthly wafer starts: 40,000  
Good wafers @ 45% yield = 18,000 wafers/month  
Good wafers @ 85% yield = 34,000 wafers/month  

**Additional good wafers per month:** 16,000  

Assuming $20,000 ASP per good wafer (typical for 1.4 nm logic),  
**Additional monthly revenue:** 16,000 × $20,000 = **$320M/month**  
→ **$3.84B per year** additional revenue from same fab.

---

### 3. Operating Cost Differences (Consumables)

| Item | MOR | RDC | Difference |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Resist consumption (L/month) | ≈8,000 | ≈4,400 (higher throughput) | –3,600 L |
| Resist cost per L | $2,000 | $2,500 (ramped) | +$500 |
| **Monthly resist cost** | $16.0M | $11.0M | **‑$5.0M** |
| Developer & other chemicals | $2M | $2M | $0 |
| **Total consumables** | $18M | $13M | **‑$5M/month** |

→ **$60M annual savings** in resist & chemicals.

---

### 4. Energy & Facility Costs (per scanner)

Higher throughput = same energy for more wafers. RDC reduces number of scanners, so total energy cost drops proportionally.

- Energy + maintenance per scanner: ~$10M/year  
- Baseline: 9 scanners → $90M/year  
- RDC: 4 scanners → $40M/year  

**Savings:** **$50M/year**

---

### Net Financial Impact (Annual) for a Single Fab

| Benefit Category | Annual Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Avoided scanner CAPEX (amortised 5 years) | $400M |
| Additional revenue from yield | $3,840M |
| Consumables savings | $60M |
| Energy & facilities savings | $50M |
| **Total annual benefit** | **≈ $4.35B** |
| *Minus incremental resist R&D amortisation* | (– $10M) |
| **Net benefit** | **> $4.3B per year per fab** |

> Even with pessimistic assumptions, the payback period for a fab adopting RDC‑Resist is **less than one month**.

---

### Value Capture for ASML (Equipment Maker)

By enabling 2.3× wafer output per scanner, RDC-Resist directly increases the value proposition of the EXE:5200B. If ASML were to bundle or recommend RDC, they could:

- Justify higher system pricing (additional $50–100M per unit)  
- Sell more scanners to greenfield fabs (since each fab needs fewer units, but total market grows)  
- Protect their roadmap against competing lithography (e‑beam, nanoimprint)

**Estimated additional gross profit for ASML over 5 years:** $5–10B across 50–100 systems.

---

*Conclusion: RDC-Resist is not just a chemical improvement – it is a **lithography economics multiplier**.*
