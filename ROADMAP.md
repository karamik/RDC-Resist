# RDC-Resist Development Roadmap
## From Lab to Fab – TRL 4 → TRL 7

*Estimated timeline and budget assuming industrial partnership.*

### Current Position (TRL 4)
- ✅ Synthesis & characterisation of RDEA‑active Sn clusters
- ✅ Proof of RDEA mechanism (time‑resolved photoemission, mass spectrometry)
- ✅ Spin coating and basic patterning on pilot EUV tool (limited statistics)
- ✅ Provisional patent

---

### Phase 1: Process Integration & Tuning (TRL 5)  
**Duration:** 6–9 months | **Budget:** $2.5–4.0M

| Activity | Responsible | Deliverable |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Optimise PAB/PEB for standard track | Partner fab / IMEC | Process window >20% |
| Scale synthesis to 10 kg/batch | Chemical partner | Pilot plant validation |
| Stochastic defect mapping (30k wafers) | IMEC High‑NA lab | D₀ <0.1 cm⁻² |

**Milestone:** Pass IMEC’s “High‑NA Resist Qualification” gate

---

### Phase 2: High‑NA Scanner Validation (TRL 6)  
**Duration:** 9–12 months | **Budget:** $5–8M

| Activity | Responsible | Deliverable |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Expose on Twinscan EXE:5200B (ASML) | ASML / IMEC | Full dose‑matrix, CD uniformity |
| Etch resistance & strip test | Foundry partner | <5% critical dimension loss |
| Defect inspection (e‑beam, brightfield) | Third‑party lab | D₀ <0.05 cm⁻², LWR <0.95 nm |

**Milestone:** Qualification for 1.4 nm logic node (N2 / 14A)

---

### Phase 3: Commercial Launch & Volume Ramp (TRL 7)  
**Duration:** 12–18 months | **Budget:** $15–25M (including capex)

| Activity | Responsible | Deliverable |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| High‑volume manufacturing (HVM) synthesis | Chemical partner (JSR/TOK/DuPont) | >100 kg/month capacity |
| Customer qualification (TSMC, Intel, Samsung) | Foundry | Production release on ≥2 fabs |
| Second‑source development | Optional | Alternate metal core (Zr, Hf) |

**Milestone:** First revenue from resist sales

---

### Total Investment to First Commercial Shipment: **$22–37M**  
*ROI break‑even: <18 months after launch (based on yield improvements alone)*
