# Technical Abstract: RDC-Resist
## Quantum‑Driven Resist for Sub‑2nm High‑NA EUV Lithography

**Version:** 1.0 (Public, pre‑NDA)  
**Classification:** Non‑Confidential Summary

---

### The Problem
High‑NA EUV (0.55 NA) enables sub‑2nm nodes but suffers from **stochastic defects** caused by uncontrolled secondary electrons (0–10 eV). Current resists require either impractically high doses (>70 mJ/cm²) or produce unacceptable line‑width roughness (>1.5 nm) and defect densities >0.5 cm⁻². This threatens the economic viability of scanners like ASML Twinscan EXE:5200B.

### The Solution – RDEA Mechanism
RDC-Resist introduces a **deterministic electron capture pathway** – Resonant Dissociative Electron Attachment (RDEA). Engineered organometallic clusters (Sn‑based) functionalised with low‑energy electron traps capture stray secondary electrons within <1 nm of their generation point. Capture triggers an immediate, local solubility switch, eliminating proximity blur.

### Key Achievements (Lab Scale)
- **LWR < 0.95 nm** at 35–45 mJ/cm² (2× wider process window vs MOR)
- **Stochastic defect density** reduced by >10×
- **Standard track compatibility** (TEL CLEAN TRACK™, 2.38% TMAH developer)
- **One‑pot scalable synthesis** (>90% yield, >1 kg batches)
